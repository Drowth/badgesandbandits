The Law of the Land

Badges & Bandits is a Wild West social deduction card game full of bluffing, bullets, and betrayal. Here’s how it’s played:

1. Setup

  • Each player receives a secret Role Card (Law or Bandit).
  • One Law player is secretly the Sheriff — protect them at all costs.
  • The Bandit Ringleader secretly chooses which players ride with them as fellow Bandits.
  • Place all Location Cards in the center of the table.
  • Shuffle the Action Deck and deal each player 3 cards.
The Twist At the start of the game, the Ringleader secretly chooses who rides with them as Bandits.

No one else knows for sure who’s in the gang — until the bullets fly.

2. Game Rounds

The game is played in rounds, each with three steps:

  • Choose: Secretly select and place an Action Card face-down.
  • Reveal: Everyone flips their cards at the same time.
  • Resolve: Apply the effects of Actions, Locations, and Items.

3. Actions

  • Heist: Bandits can steal and generate Heist Tokens (Badges may bluff).
  • Buy: Draw Item Cards from the Merchant.
  • Gather: Collect resources to aid the town.
  • Rest: Lose suspicion and recover.
  • Stakeout: Peek at hidden cards and share (or lie about) what you saw.
Duel Showdown When accusations boil over, pistols are drawn.

A bystander lays down a secret combo of Action Cards in the center.
The duelists lock eyes, finger‑guns raised.

A countdown begins: 5...4...3...2...1... FIRE!

Both players scramble to rebuild the combo from their decks while holding their aim.
The first to slam down the right set and call their shot wins.

The loser pays the price — either dropping dead on the dusty street or losing their last bit of armor.

5. Gallows Event

After every three rounds, the town votes on the most suspicious player. If they are hanged, their true role is revealed for all to see. Should the Bandit Ringleader be caught, it’s game over for the gang. But beware — if the Sheriff swings, the town’s defenses crumble.

6. Winning

  • Law-Aligned Win: If the Bandit Ringleader is hanged at the Gallows, or if the town survives with a positive Law & Order Tracker by completing enough Perils of the Frontier (quests).
  • Bandits Win: If the Sheriff is hanged by mistake, if the town’s Law & Order collapses from too many failed Perils, or if the Ringleader survives until the end.
