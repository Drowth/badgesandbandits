How to Play

Welcome to Badges & Bandits — a gritty, social deduction card game about bluffing, bullets, and betrayal.

Why I Made This Game

I’ve always loved games like Coup, Bang!, and Secret Hitler — ones full of second guessing, accusations, and betrayal. I wanted to create something with that same energy, but set in the Wild West. A place where you don’t know who to trust, and every smile might hide a pistol.

This game is my first attempt at designing something from the ground up — and with the help of my playgroup, it’s already sparked showdowns, chaos, and plenty of “you what?!” moments. If you enjoy out-bluffing your mates, this one’s for you.

Quick Summary

  • You're either a Badge (Sheriff, Deputy, Marshal’s Courier) or a Bandit (or Ringleader)
  • Choose a location, play an action card, resolve chaos
  • After Rounds 3 & 5, players vote to hang someone
  • Bandits win by pulling off 3 Heists or killing the Sheriff
  • Badges win by hanging the Ringleader (after ≥1 Heist) or stopping all Heists

Setup

  1. Deal 1 Role Card to each player (face down)
  2. Use either Standard Roles or the Ringleader Recruitment phase (see below)
  3. Place 5 Location Cards in a central pool
  4. Each player receives 6 Action Cards
  5. Shuffle and place the Item Deck
  6. Set Suspicion, Damage, Round Tracker, and Heist Token Pile on the table

Role Options

  • Ringleader: Head of the Bandits. Must be hanged to lose.
  • Bandit: Standard outlaw. Can perform Heists and defend allies.
  • Sheriff: Law’s leader. Must be protected.
  • Deputy: Law-aligned, cannot Heist.
  • Marshal's Courier: Once per game, may change locations after all actions are revealed.

Ringleader Recruitment Phase

Only the Ringleader knows their role at first. All others have law aligned cards.

  1. All players close eyes with their role cards out in front of them
  2. The Ringleader opens their eyes and silently replaces 1–2 role cards with Bandit roles
  3. Everyone reopens their eyes and checks their (maybe new) role card, obviously everyone should be shocked and exclaim they have been changed, because thats just funny. (probably)

“Everyone, close your eyes and hold out your hand. Ringleader, open your eyes… select your allies… now close them. Everyone may open their eyes.”

Round Structure

  1. 1. Location Draft: Players pick a location (round-robin)
  2. 2. Play Actions: Secretly play one card (Heist, Buy, Shoot, etc.)
  3. 3. Reveal & Resolve: Location by location, cards are flipped and effects are resolved
  4. 4. Apply Effects: Heists, Suspicion, Damage, Items
  5. 5. Gallows Phase: After Rounds 3 & 5 — vote to hang someone

Victory Conditions

  • Bandits Win: 3 Heists succeed or the Sheriff dies
  • Badges Win: Ringleader is hanged (after ≥1 Heist) or no Heists by Round 5
  • Optional: In games with 6+ players, Ringleader must be hanged

Snake Oil Curses (Item Cards)

  • Grizzly Growl: You must growl like a bear before speaking. Forget? Gain 1 Suspicion.
  • Rattlesnake Reflex: Flinch or mime drawing a pistol every time someone says your name. Fail? Gain 1 Suspicion.
  • Cactus Cramp: Stops a player from playing “Buy” next round.
  • Thuthpithuthly Happy Fever: Speak with an exaggerated lisp for the rest of the game. Drop the act? Anyone can assign you Suspicion.

Duels

Sometimes two players will find themselves in a 1v1 standoff. When that happens, the table pauses and a Duel Showdown begins.

  1. Dealer of Fate: The first player eliminated from the game or the last player to scratch their nose becomes the Dealer of Fate. They take the two Duel Action Cards and lay them facedown in a random order.
  2. Face-Off: The two dueling players raise their finger pistols and lock eyes. The Dealer counts down loud and clear: “5… 4… 3… 2… 1… DRAW!”
  3. Quickdraw: At “DRAW!”, both duelists must slam their hands down, grab the Duel Cards, and arrange them in the correct order (Attack → Resolution) as fast as possible.
  4. Winner: The first duelist to correctly lay out the order wins the duel. If both complete at the exact same time, the Dealer of Fate chooses the winner.

Optional Variant: If your group prefers less theatrics, simply have both duelists flip one card each simultaneously instead of performing the Quickdraw.

Action: Stakeout

As an action, a player may choose to play Stakeout. This allows the player to secretly peek at 2 face-down Action Cards in the exact order they are currently arranged.

  • The player may then return the cards in the same order.
  • They may share their findings with the table — truthfully, deceptively, or not at all.
  • Other players must decide whether to believe them, adding a new layer of bluff and mistrust.

Remember: The power of Stakeout lies as much in what you say as in what you actually saw.
